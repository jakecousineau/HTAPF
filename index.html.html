About Face Your Financial Fears

Personal finance, finally made personal.

Is getting your finances in order always on your to-do list — but never quite getting done? Do you feel guilty about your money habits, overwhelmed by the thought of addressing them?

Jake Cousineau's Face Your Financial Fears helps you understand your financial situation, provides actionable steps to address your money problems, and sets measurable goals that are relevant to you.

Drawing on years of teaching personal finance in the high school classroom — and valuable life experience as a young professional — Jake covers everything from compound interest and mutual funds to Roth IRAs and insurance deductibles.

What You'll Learn

Topics covered in Face Your Financial Fears

Each chapter contains straightforward explanations, practical examples, revealing anecdotes, and hands-on tools to jump-start your personal financial journey.

Banking Basics

Understand checking vs. savings, interest rates, and how to choose the right bank account for your life stage.

Investing Fundamentals

Demystify stocks, index funds, and mutual funds — and understand why starting early makes all the difference.

Retirement Planning

Roth IRAs, 401(k)s, and compound interest explained in plain language, with action steps you can take today.

Insurance & Risk

Health, auto, renters, and life insurance — what deductibles actually mean and how to avoid being underinsured.

Credit & Debt

Build and protect your credit score, manage debt strategically, and understand when borrowing makes sense.

Your Financial Roadmap

Set measurable, personalized goals and build a budget that actually works for your real life — not a textbook example.

Who is this book for?
While I hope people will read this book when they are young, How to Adult is meant for anyone who needs a foundation in personal finance. Although it covers simpler topics like basic banking, it also covers Roth IRAs and index funds — topics the majority of adults can't competently explain.
